Case study: Hyatt Regency — brief chronology

  1. Design change in hanger rod connections during shop fabrication.
  2. Load path changed so a single connection carried much larger load than intended.
  3. During a crowded event, the walkways failed and many lives were lost.

Engineering takeaway: Changes in shop drawings must be reviewed for structural load paths; third-party checks and clear responsibility allocation prevent such mistakes.

Common Causes & Failure Mechanisms

  • Poor design or calculation errors (incorrect assumptions, incomplete load cases)
  • Inadequate connections and detailing
  • Material defects and quality control failures
  • Corrosion, fatigue and lack of maintenance
  • Unexpected loading (overloads, impact, aerodynamic effects)
  • Construction errors and deviations from design
  • Insufficient inspection, testing and asset management

Important Formulas & Quick Checks

Below are commonly used checks and formulas (summary only — always consult codes for design):

  • Axial stress: σ = P / A
  • Bending stress: σ = M y / I
  • Shear stress (average): τ = V / A
  • Euler buckling load (pinned ends): Pcr = (π^2 E I) / (L^2)
  • Fatigue design: use S–N curves and cumulative damage (Miner's rule) for repetitive loads
  • Connection check: ensure bolt/plate capacities + appropriate partial safety factors (γ)

Always run serviceability (deflection), ultimate limit state and connection verifications for safety.

Practice: Interview Questions & MCQs

Short interview prompts

  • Explain the difference between ductile and brittle failure with examples.
  • What is a load path? Give an example where the load path changed and caused a failure.
  • How do you assess existing bridges for continued safety?
  • Describe how corrosion affects prestressed concrete.

MCQs (quick revision)

Q1. The Tacoma Narrows Bridge collapse was primarily caused by:
  1. Excessive live load
  2. Aeroelastic flutter
  3. Soil liquefaction
  4. Poor concrete quality

Answer: B (Aeroelastic flutter)

Q2. A common symptom of fatigue in steel members is:
  1. Gradual deflection increase only
  2. Sudden brittle fracture without prior cracks
  3. Crack initiation and propagation from stress concentrators
  4. Immediate yielding under service load

Answer: C (Crack initiation and propagation from stress concentrators)
